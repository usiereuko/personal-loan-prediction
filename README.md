# Personal Loan Prediction

## Overview
This project analyzes customer demographic, financial, and banking relationship data to predict whether a customer is likely to accept a personal loan offer. Machine learning classification techniques were used to identify key factors influencing loan acceptance and support targeted marketing strategies.

## Business Objective
AllLife Bank aims to increase adoption of its personal loan product among existing customers. The objective of this project is to build a predictive model that helps identify customers with a high likelihood of purchasing a personal loan while improving marketing efficiency and customer targeting.

## Skills and Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Scikit-learn
- Classification Models

- Model Evaluation

## Models Evaluated
- Logistic Regression
- Decision Tree Classifier
- Pre-pruning and Post-pruning Techniques

## Final Model Selection
The post-pruning Decision Tree model was selected as the final model due to its strong recall performance, balanced accuracy, and stable generalization across datasets.

## Key Insights
- Income, average credit card spending (CCAvg), and CD account ownership were among the strongest predictors of personal loan acceptance.
- Customers with higher income levels and active banking relationships showed a greater likelihood of accepting loan offers.
- The model demonstrated strong ability to identify high-potential customers for targeted marketing campaigns.

## Business Recommendations
- Focus marketing efforts on customers with higher income levels and active credit usage.
- Use predictive insights to improve customer segmentation and campaign efficiency.
- Integrate the model into marketing workflows to support personalized loan targeting.
